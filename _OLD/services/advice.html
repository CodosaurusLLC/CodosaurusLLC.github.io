What Is It?

This means discussing to determine the current and desired situations, and what tools, techniques, processes, etc. would be most effective to bridge that gap (within a reasonable budget).

Why Do It?

Mainly, to help your team work efficiently, rather than being hobbled by an inefficient process, bad tools, etc.  Yes, it's a bad craftsman who blames his tools... but a good craftsman will still do better with good tools (and techniques, and processes).

How Does It Work?

Part 1: Arranging

First, you send me some generic information.  I email you back, asking some more questions.  Some of these are generic, and some are follow-ups to what you said.  There may be some more back-and-forth.  This is all to make sure that we make best use of our limited time together.

If we determine that my advice services are a good fit for you, we discuss to determine how much time of observation and discussion you need.  After you send in a 50% deposit, we set a date.

Part 2: Discussing

When that date comes, you and I, and possibly other people, discuss such things as:

  • what challenges your team is facing,
  • what you'd like the situation to be,
  • what unique strengths and weaknesses your team has,
  • what sorts of things you're planning to have them work on,
  • what you're doing now,
  • what else you've tried,
  • how that worked,
  • what budget you have for personnel, tools, and infrastructure,

and so on.  With the larger sizes, I may also observe your current processes, meetings, tools, etc. in action, especially any planning and review meetings (even relevant ones where the developers are not present).

About halfway through, we'll check our progress, to see if you feel that we are on track to cover everything you need.  If not, we'll discuss options such as covering a broader area but in less depth, staying more focused, changing direction, taking more time now (if I'm available), scheduling additional sessions for later, and so on.

At the end, we'll check coverage again, to see if we're really done discussing, or if we need to take more time now or schedule additional sessions.  We may need that to go into more depth, examine new ideas, discuss something we skipped, or other reasons.

You may also have me temporarily join your team, so as to experience the situation from within.  Even without knowing the specific technology stack, I can observe a lot about how your team works -- including how they "onboard" a new person, which can be critical to success.

Part 3: Delivering

After our discussion, I do some more research to close up any gaps, and maybe ask you some more questions by phone or email.  Within about a week or two, if you didn't go for the "small" size, I will send you a report, containing recommendations about processes, techniques, tools, etc. tailored to your staff, projects, etc.

The second half of the payment is due 20 days after billing (which is usually right after delivery of the report).  There are penalties for late payment and discounts for early payment.

How Long Does It Take?

As implied above, we need to discuss that.  The basic size will be influenced mainly by how many people I need to interview and how many hours of meetings I need to observe.  Then add any weeks that you like me to work as part of your team.  Finally, I will need about as long as the discussions, to finish my research and write up your report.

How Much Does It Cost?

It comes in three sizes (though you can buy multiples), as follows:

Hour

You and I, and nobody else, talk remotely, for one hour straight.  You get no report, and it costs $499 for the first hour and $399 for each additional hour.  There is a limit of eight per day and there will be breaks between each pair.

Day

We talk, and/or I listen in on your meetings, for one workday, up to nine hours.  We do this remotely, or, when there is no pandemic, at your office.  In each talking stretch, I may talk with you, or some other person you designate, or both.  (You may designate different people for each stretch.)  You get a rather rough report, and it costs you $4,999 for the first day and $3,999 for each additional day.  (Yes, that's more than doing it hour by hour, versus the discount you were probably expecting.  That's mainly because of the report.)

Week

This works mostly as in the "day" version, repeated for a whole work-week, Monday to Friday.  During the talking stretches, you may designate up to two other people at a time for me to talk to without you.  You get a fairly detailed report, and it costs you $19,999 for the first week and $14,999 for each additional week.

Also, you may have me actually join your team, so as to see your situation from the inside.  Doing this on a full-time basis is limited to four weeks, but this is also available as half-time, for $7,999 per week.

What Else Can I Get?

After you've had a chance to review the report, we can discuss whether you want some additional services, for additional prices we will discuss.  Mainly:

  • If I'm available, you may ask to have me continue, including either more interviews and such, or working as part of your team, including ongoing process improvement, mentoring (especially of junior developers), etc.  We can do this on a part-time basis, retainer, or various other arrangements.
  • We can arrange a "check-up" in the future, where I will look at any changes in your goals, staff, types of projects, technologies, etc.
  • Of course I'm happy to "cross-sell" you on my training and project roadmapping services.  :-)
  • If there are other add-on services you would like, we can discuss them then.

If there's anything else you need, or you're ready to submit your inquiry, go ahead and contact me.
