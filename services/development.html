The Basics

I mostly write "back-end" software, command-line programs, and other such things, where the emphasis is on correctness, robustness, efficiency, etc. rather than visual design.  I can do basic front-end work, but it is not at all my forte; in particular, do not expect it to look pretty.  So, if your project is "front-end", I'm out; if it's "full-stack", I might not be the best choice (unless it's rather back-end heavy); but if it's "back-end", I'm your dinosaur man!

As for specific technologies, most of my work lately has been in Ruby, including on Rails, in Sinatra, and non-web.  I'm now learning Elixir (and Phoenix) and would like to get some experience in those.  In the past I've done tons of C and SQL, some Python, and many other languages.  For the right opportunity, I'll learn almost anything.  But I don't do Windows.  See the Skills page for more details.

Fixed Price Projects

I may make a fixed-price bid on your project, but only if you have used my roadmapping services for it first.  Project-based work is done remotely (barring any meetings we agree on before starting), on the schedule we agree on for completion and any other milestones.

I generally require a 50% deposit up front, and the rest net-20.  For long projects, we may negotiate other milestone payments.

Buying My Time

If you just want me to join your team and program, okay.  However, you must already have good software engineering processes and tooling set up.  (If you don't, perhaps you need my advice service first, and time to act on it!)  Also, I won't be doing any of my other services for you on a time basis, and the effort I put into leveling-up the rest of your team will be minimal and mainly ad-hoc.

I generally don't do hours, let alone partial ones, strongly preferring days or weeks.  This saves both of us overhead and conflict.  You should not have to worry about how many hours something takes me, so long as I deliver on schedule and budget.  As Jonathan Stark says, Hourly Billing Is Nuts!

My rate for remote work, for approximately the Level of Effort (LoE) of one Full Time Employee/Equivalent (FTE), is $1,099 per day or $4,999 per week.  I am also open to a partial-FTE LoE, larger time units, etc.  If you insist on counting hours, it's $199 per hour or part thereof, and more if you want me to track time even more tightly than that.

Onsite work costs much more.  Ongoing onsite work will only be done within about 15 miles of Fairfax, Virginia.

All travel and related time counts, including to and from ongoing onsite work.

Other Notes

  • I will fix for free anything that we both agree is a bug in software I wrote.  (This will usually require that there be requirements, or at least approved tickets or approved tests.  There is a deadline of one month after your acceptance or two months after my submission, whichever comes first.)
  • I will learn for free anything that I think I will likely use on later contracts for other clients.
  • I have a TS (though not the various add-ons).  If you don't know what I mean, I won't tell you, but you don't need it.  (Yes it's somewhat odd to mention in combo with working remotely, but it can be beneficial for on-site visits.)
  • I am not open to "permanent" employment, changing careers, relocation, spending months on the road, or working for equity.  Please do not contact me about such things; I will treat it as spam.

If there's anything else you need, or you're ready to submit your inquiry, go ahead and contact me.
